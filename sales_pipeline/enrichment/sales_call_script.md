# Americal Patrol — Sales Call Script for Property Managers

## Before You Call

- **Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM (highest answer rates)
- **Goal of the call:** Book a 15-minute in-person meeting or phone call. Do NOT try to close a deal on the first call.
- **Mindset:** You're offering to help, not selling. Property managers deal with security problems whether they hire you or not — you're the solution.
- **Have ready:** The company's name, what they manage, and one personalized detail (from the research notes below)

---

## The Script

### OPENING (10 seconds — get past the gatekeeper reflex)

> "Hi {{NAME}}, this is Don Burt with Americal Patrol. I'm a local security company here in Oxnard — do you have 30 seconds?"

**Why this works:** Saying your name and company first builds trust. Asking for "30 seconds" (not "a few minutes") respects their time and almost always gets a yes.

**If they say "I'm busy":**
> "Totally understand — when's a better time for a quick 2-minute call? I'll be brief, I promise."

**If gatekeeper answers:**
> "Hi, I'm Don Burt with Americal Patrol — I was hoping to speak with {{NAME}} about security services for their managed properties. Is this a good time to reach them, or is there a better number?"

---

### BRIDGE (15 seconds — why you're calling, with a personalized hook)

Pick the hook that fits the company:

**For HOA managers (CPM, RowCal, Coast/Ventura County HOA, Powerstone):**
> "I know you manage HOA communities across Ventura County, and I wanted to reach out because we work with several HOA boards in the area. Security keeps coming up at board meetings, and I thought it'd be worth introducing myself as a local option."

**For commercial/industrial managers (Becker Group, Ventura Property Group):**
> "I know you manage commercial properties across Ventura County, and I'm calling because we provide patrol services for commercial and industrial sites in the same areas. Wanted to introduce myself as a local resource."

**For multi-family residential (Alert/COVA, Rincon, Esquire, RPM):**
> "I know you manage rental properties in {{CITY}}, and I'm reaching out because apartment communities are where we see the biggest demand for patrol right now — parking lot issues, after-hours incidents, that kind of thing."

**For Alert Management (David Covarrubias) — special hometown hook:**
> "David, I know your father started Alert Management back in 1960 — that's incredible. We've been in Oxnard since '86 ourselves. I thought two Oxnard originals should at least know each other, and I think there might be a good fit between what you do and what we do."

---

### DISCOVERY (60-90 seconds — ask questions, LISTEN more than you talk)

> "I don't want to pitch you — I'd rather learn a little about what you're dealing with. Can I ask you a couple quick questions?"

**Question 1 — Open the door:**
> "Are any of your properties currently using security patrol, or has it come up with any of your boards/owners recently?"

**If YES (they have security):**
> "Got it — are you happy with the service you're getting? One thing we hear a lot is that the bigger national companies rotate guards constantly and the reports are thin. Is that something you've experienced?"

**If NO (no security currently):**
> "That's pretty common. What do you do currently when there's an after-hours incident at one of your properties — a break-in, trespassing, something like that? Does your team handle it, or...?"

**Question 2 — Identify pain:**
> "What would you say is the biggest security-related headache across your properties right now? Is it vandalism, trespassing, parking issues, after-hours calls — what keeps coming up?"

**Question 3 — Quantify the problem (if they share a pain point):**
> "How often does that happen? Weekly? Monthly? And when it does, what does it cost you in terms of time, repairs, or tenant complaints?"

---

### VALUE STATEMENT (30 seconds — connect YOUR solution to THEIR pain)

Based on what they shared, pick the response that fits:

**If their pain is after-hours incidents / emergency calls:**
> "That's exactly what our patrol is designed to handle. Our officers are on-site during the hours when problems happen — evenings, nights, weekends. When something comes up, they handle it in real time and document everything, which means fewer 2 AM phone calls to your team."

**If their pain is vandalism / property damage:**
> "Visible patrol is the single most effective deterrent for that. When people see a marked patrol vehicle and a uniformed officer checking the property, they move on. Our clients typically see vandalism and property damage drop significantly within the first month."

**If their pain is tenant / resident complaints about safety:**
> "That's one of the main reasons boards hire us. When residents see a patrol officer they recognize making regular rounds, it changes the entire feel of the community. We've had board members tell us their complaint volume dropped by half after we started."

**If their pain is liability / legal exposure:**
> "You're thinking about this the right way. Every patrol we run is GPS-tracked with timestamped checkpoints and detailed reports. If a liability claim ever comes up, you have documented proof that reasonable security measures were in place. It's the kind of evidence that changes the outcome of a premises liability case."

---

### THE ASK (15 seconds — book the meeting, not the sale)

> "Look, I don't want to take up your whole morning. What I'd love to do is grab 15 minutes with you — coffee's on me — and I can show you exactly what our patrol reports look like and how we work with property managers. No pressure, no hard sell. If there's a fit, great. If not, at least you'll know who to call if security ever comes up."

> "Would {{DAY}} or {{DAY}} work for a quick meeting?"

**Always offer two specific days** — don't say "sometime next week" (too vague, easy to defer).

---

### OBJECTION HANDLING

**"We're not looking for security right now."**
> "Totally fair — most of the property managers I work with weren't looking either until something happened. I'm not trying to sell you today. I just want to introduce myself so that when it does come up — and with {{NUMBER}} properties, it usually does — you have a local option you already know and trust. Can I send you a one-page overview to keep on file?"

**"We already have a security company."**
> "Good — security's important, so I'm glad you have someone. Out of curiosity, are you getting detailed patrol reports with GPS verification and photo documentation? That's where we hear the most frustration with the bigger firms — the reports are either thin or non-existent. If you're ever looking for a comparison quote, I'd be happy to put one together."

**"What does it cost?"**
> "It depends on the property — number of units, how many hours of coverage, the patrol route. Rather than throw out a number that might not apply, I'd love to learn more about the specific property so I can put together an accurate quote. Can we set up a quick meeting to walk through it?"

**"Send me some information."**
> "Happy to — what's the best email? I'll send a one-page overview and a sample patrol report so you can see exactly what we deliver. And I'll follow up in a few days to see if you have any questions. Sound good?"

**"I need to talk to the board / my owners."**
> "Of course — that makes total sense. Would it help if I put together a short presentation you could bring to the next board meeting? I've done that for other community managers and it makes the conversation a lot easier. When's the next meeting?"

**"We had a bad experience with a security company before."**
> "I hear that more than you'd think, and honestly, that's part of why we exist. The big national firms rotate guards constantly — nobody ever gets to know your property. We assign named officers to specific properties, and I personally oversee every account. What specifically went wrong last time? I'd like to make sure I can address that directly."

---

### CLOSING / WRAP-UP

**If they agree to meet:**
> "Great — I'll send you a calendar invite for {{DAY}} at {{TIME}}. Looking forward to it, {{NAME}}. Thanks for your time."

**If they say "send info first":**
> "Will do — I'll get that over to you today. I'll follow up {{DAY}} to see if you had a chance to look at it. Thanks, {{NAME}}."

**If it's a clear no:**
> "I appreciate your time, {{NAME}}. If security ever comes up with one of your properties, I hope you'll give us a call. I'll leave you my number — it's (805) 515-3834. Have a great day."
